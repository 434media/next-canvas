# Digital Canvas — Partner Alignment

**Internal working doc for DevSA, Alamo Angels, and 434 Media. Aligns roles, value flow, and v1 scope before cohort 1 launches.**

---

## Thesis

San Antonio has the talent, the verticals, and the capital — but no program layer connecting them. Digital Canvas is that layer: AI-native cohorts running against industry-authored pain points, ending in demo days to the Alamo Angels network.

We are not building YC. We are connecting two pipelines that already exist — DevSA's community and Alamo Angels' investor base — with an underwriter-funded program in between.

---

## Five-way value loop

| Stakeholder | Gives | Gets |
|---|---|---|
| Builders | Time, commitment to demo | Free workshop, AI-tool fluency, space, warm investor intros |
| Underwriters | Cohort funding | Named vertical, talent pipeline, first-look, branded media |
| Angels / Investors | Demo day attendance | Pre-curated dealflow, no sourcing cost |
| DevSA | Workshop hosting, audience reach, space | Program content fits mission, sponsor visibility, ecosystem leadership |
| 434 Media | Operations, media, underwriter sales | Program ownership, media deliverables, ecosystem positioning |

If any column stops earning, the loop breaks. Test each one quarterly.

---

## Partner roles

**DevSA**
- Hosts workshop weekend at a partner venue
- Promotes cohorts through DevSA social channels and shared community calendar
- Opens downtown coworking space to committed cohort for the six-week bridge
- Wraps program in existing "find your people, build your future" message

**Alamo Angels**
- Provides pitch coaching session in bridge week 4
- Convenes investor audience for demo day
- Continues post-demo conversations through standard rolling application path

**434 Media / Marcos Resendez**
- Recruits and closes cohort underwriters
- Coordinates operations across partners
- Produces media (recap, builder profiles, demo footage)
- Owns Digital Canvas brand and entity

---

## Cadence (v1 target)

- **Cohorts per year:** 2–3 (allows underwriter recruitment runway between)
- **Workshop:** 1 weekend
- **Bridge:** 6 weeks
- **Demo day:** 1 evening
- **Total cohort window:** ~7 weeks active, 2–3 weeks recovery / next-cohort prep

---

## Money flow

To be ratified before cohort 1:

- Underwriter check lands with: _____ (TBD — likely 434 Media as program operator)
- DevSA receives: _____ (space + audience contribution — flat fee or rev share)
- Alamo Angels receives: _____ (pitch coaching contribution — flat fee or in-kind)
- 434 Media retains: _____ (operations, media, sales overhead)

A simple flat-allocation model is preferable to rev share in v1 — keeps incentives clean and reporting light.

---

## v1 scope (what's in)

- One cohort, one vertical (cyber or military recommended for SA fit + underwriter density)
- Free workshop + 6-week bridge + demo day
- Pitch coaching, sponsor office hour, investor social
- Media recap

## v1 scope (what's out — explicitly deferred)

- Bounty program / localized Upwork (post-cohort-3 at earliest)
- Multi-vertical concurrent cohorts
- Paid consultancy sales motion (can be inbound-only for v1, not a structured outbound)
- International or out-of-SA expansion

---

## Cohort 1 success metrics

Set the bar low enough to learn, high enough to matter:

- 8–12 committed builders make it to demo day
- ≥1 investor-builder follow-up conversation per builder, on average
- ≥1 underwriter renews or refers for cohort 2
- Net Promoter from builders ≥ 8/10
- Media assets delivered to underwriter on time

---

## Decision points needing alignment before launch

1. Which vertical for cohort 1? (Cyber leads on underwriter density; military leads on SA distinctiveness)
2. Underwriter price band and what's included
3. Money flow allocation (above)
4. Who is Marcos's #2 by cohort 3 to de-risk key-person dependency
5. Workshop dates, demo day date, and underwriter close deadline
